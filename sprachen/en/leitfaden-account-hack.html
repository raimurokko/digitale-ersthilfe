My account was hacked — what now?

Last reviewed: 13 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: If you suddenly cannot sign in or you see strange activity, your account has probably been taken over. Act fast in 4 steps: get the account back, secure it, limit the damage, file a police report.

First aid:

  • Use a different device if you think your computer or phone is infected.
  • Never change passwords through links in emails or text messages — open the official website by hand.
  • If money is affected: call the bank at once and block the card (116 116).
01

Get your account back

First, try to get access to the account again. All big providers have a „forgot password“ or „recover account“ function for this.

Help pages of the main providers:

  • Google — recover a hacked account ↗
  • Apple — recover your Apple ID ↗
  • Microsoft — account recovery ↗
  • Facebook — report a hacked account ↗
  • Instagram — report a hacked account ↗

Tip: Answer the recovery questions as fully as you can. Give old passwords that you once used — this helps the provider recognise you as the rightful owner.

02

Secure your account

Secure it right after recovery, before the attackers get back in.

  • New, strong password: At least 12 characters, not used anywhere else. Use a password manager.
  • Turn on two-factor authentication (2FA): Best with an authenticator app (for example Aegis or 2FAS), not by SMS.
  • Check the recovery email and phone number: Attackers often change these. Make sure only you have access.
  • Sign out active sessions: In the settings of every service there is a „sign out all devices“ option.
  • Check email forwarding rules: Attackers often set up secret forwarding.
  • Check connected apps: Remove unknown third-party apps that have access.
03

Limit the damage

  • Warn your contacts: Tell friends and family — attackers often send scam messages in your name.
  • Bank & cards: Check your account statements from the last weeks. If you see suspicious charges: call the bank and block the card (116 116).
  • Check other accounts: Did you use the same password somewhere else? Change it there too.
  • Data leak check: Check at HPI Identity Leak Checker ↗ or Have I Been Pwned ↗ which of your data has leaked.
  • Check your device for malware: Run an antivirus scan. If in doubt, set the system up fresh.

If your identity is stolen: Report the identity misuse to the SCHUFA and get a free self-disclosure (a copy of your data under Art. 15 DSGVO ↗). More detail: Identity theft guide.

Enforce your rights: With the free data-protection template letters from the Verbraucherzentrale (consumer advice centre) ↗ you can ask what data is stored and demand its deletion (DSGVO).

04

File a police report

Even if the damage seems small: A police report helps to reveal the pattern and protect others.

  • Use the police online station (Online-Wache) ↗ or go to a police station.
  • Relevant offences: § 202a StGB ↗ (spying out data), § 202c StGB ↗ (preparing to spy out data), § 263a StGB ↗ (computer fraud) when money is affected.
  • Bring screenshots, timestamps, email headers and transaction records.
  • You can also report the incident to the BSI: 0800 274 1000.

Common questions

How can I tell my account was hacked?

Typical signs: you suddenly cannot sign in, the password or recovery email was changed, friends get scam messages in your name, or you see unknown logins and charges.

What should I do first?

Get your account back with the provider's „forgot password“ or „recover account“ function — from a clean device and never through links in emails or text messages. If money is affected, block the card at once by calling 116 116.

How do I secure my account after recovery?

Set a new, strong password (at least 12 characters, not used anywhere else), turn on two-factor authentication with an authenticator app instead of SMS, check the recovery email and phone number, sign out all devices, and remove secret forwarding rules and unknown connected apps.

Should I file a police report?

Yes — even for small damage, through the police online station (Online-Wache) or a station. Relevant offences are §§ 202a, 202c and 263a StGB. Bring screenshots, timestamps, email headers and evidence. You can also report the incident to the BSI (0800 274 1000).

How can I find out if my data is in a data leak?

Check your email address with the HPI Identity Leak Checker or with Have I Been Pwned. If your identity is stolen, also report the identity misuse to the SCHUFA and get a free self-disclosure (a copy of your data under Art. 15 DSGVO ↗).
