Online scams — Money gone. What can I do?

Last reviewed: 12 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: You paid and got nothing (or the wrong thing), or you fell for a fake shop or an investment scam? Now speed matters. In 4 steps: stop the payment and get your money back, save evidence, report it to the police, report the offer and protect your other accounts. It is not your fault.

First aid:

  • Act right away — with fresh bank transfers every hour counts. Call your bank first.
  • Do not pay anything more (“fees”, “taxes”, “unlocking”) to get your money or claimed profits — that is part of the scam.
  • Beware of “get your money back” offers: anyone who promises to get your lost money back is often the second scam.
01

Stop the payment and get your money back

Your chances depend a lot on how you paid — act fast.

  • Bank transfer / advance payment: Call your bank at once and try to recall the transfer. This only works in the first few hours.
  • Direct debit: Have your bank reverse it — up to 8 weeks after the debit (up to 13 months for an unauthorised direct debit).
  • Credit card: Ask your bank for a chargeback (reversal).
  • PayPal: Request buyer protection in your PayPal account under “Resolution Center” (not for “Friends and Family”).
  • Cash transfer (Western Union, MoneyGram): A recall is possible as long as the money has not been picked up yet.
  • Gave out card or account details? Block the card on 116 116.
02

Save evidence

  • Screenshots of the shop or the offer, the legal notice, the order, the payment receipt, the chat history and emails.
  • Write down the address (URL) of the shop or offer, plus the date and time.
  • Save everything together safely — you need it for the bank and for the police report.
03

Report it to the police

  • Report it to the police, or online through the Online-Wache (police online station) ↗, with the evidence you saved.
  • Possible offences: § 263 StGB (fraud), § 263a StGB (computer fraud). The police decide how to classify it.
  • Keep the case number — banks and payment services often ask for it.
04

Report the offer and protect your other accounts

  • Check and report a fake shop: with the Fakeshop-Finder from the Verbraucherzentrale (consumer advice centre) ↗; known shops are on the warning list ↗.
  • Investment or crypto scam: Check warnings from the financial regulator BaFin ↗. Warning signs: no legal notice, no BaFin licence, time pressure, payout only after further deposits.
  • Change passwords if you entered any on the site; run a data-leak check ↗.
  • Advice: The Verbraucherzentrale helps with reclaiming money and with judging the situation.

Frequently asked questions

I paid by bank transfer (in advance) — is the money gone?

A bank transfer is hard to get back, but try right away: call your bank and ask them to recall the transfer. This only works in the first few hours. The faster you act, the better.

Will I get my money back with PayPal?

If you did not use the “Friends and Family” option, PayPal buyer protection often applies — for example if the goods do not arrive or clearly do not match the description. You file the request in your PayPal account under “Resolution Center”.

I am told to pay more fees or taxes to get my money — is that true?

No. Extra demands for “fees”, “taxes” or “unlocking” are part of the scam. Do not pay anything more.

Someone offers to get my lost money back — is that trustworthy?

Usually not. Such “get your money back” offers — often claiming to act for BaFin or an authority — are frequently a second scam (recovery scam). Do not go along with it and do not pay anything.

How do I spot a fake shop?

Check the shop address with the Fakeshop-Finder from the Verbraucherzentrale (consumer advice centre). Warning signs are: only advance payment by bank transfer, prices that are unrealistically cheap, a missing or fake legal notice, and a newly registered web address.
