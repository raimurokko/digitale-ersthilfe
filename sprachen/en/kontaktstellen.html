  • No More Ransom — free decryption tools ↗

Important with ransomware: Do not pay any ransom. Disconnect the device from the network. Report it to the police. The police can help with decryption.

💳
Central card blocking
116 116
Block all bank cards and credit cards — around the clock

What you can do right now:

  • Block your card: Call 116 116 or use your bank's app.
  • Block online banking: Call your bank's emergency number (it is on the back of your bank card).
  • Unauthorised debit? You can have direct debits (money taken from your account) reversed by your bank within 8 weeks.
  • Identity theft: Set up an identity alert with SCHUFA and request a copy of your own data.
  • Check an online shop: With the fake shop finder of the Verbraucherzentrale (consumer advice centre) ↗ you can check whether a shop address is genuine before you buy.
  • Known fake shops: The warning list of the Verbraucherzentrale ↗ lists fraudulent online shops reported recently.
  • Phishing Radar of the Verbraucherzentrale — current warnings about phishing emails ↗
  • Sample letters (digital world) — free templates from the Verbraucherzentrale for objection, cancellation and complaint ↗
  • Sample letters on data protection — ask for access to and deletion of your data (DSGVO, the EU data protection law), templates from the Verbraucherzentrale ↗
  • Verbraucherzentrale — personal advice and more topics ↗
  • Bundesnetzagentur (Federal Network Agency) — complaints about phone-number misuse and spam calls ↗
  • Polizei-Beratung — prevention tips on cybercrime and fraud ↗

You can also report crimes to the police online. Every federal state has its own Online-Wache (online police station).

  • Online-Wache — file a criminal report online ↗

For serious cybercrime cases (for example ransomware attacks on companies) every federal state has a Central Contact Point for Cybercrime (ZAC) at the police. These are mainly responsible for companies and public authorities.
