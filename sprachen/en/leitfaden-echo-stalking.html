Spot spying through an Amazon Echo and save evidence

Last reviewed: 12 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: If you think someone is using an Amazon Echo (Alexa) to listen in on you, it is important to act fast and with care. This guide shows you in 4 steps how to protect yourself, save evidence and report it to the police.

Legal basis: Secretly listening in on conversations is a crime under § 201 StGB ↗ (violation of the confidentiality of the spoken word). It can be punished with up to 3 years in prison.

Important — please read first:

  • Do not confront the person you suspect before you have reported it to the police. Otherwise evidence could be deleted.
  • Do not secretly record any conversations yourself. That is the same crime (§ 201 StGB ↗) and weakens your position.
  • Your safety comes first. If you feel threatened, contact the police (110) or a support service right away.
01

Immediate protection: protect yourself and your conversations

The first step is to protect yourself right away, without the other person noticing.

What you should do now:

  • Move private conversations. Talk about important things at least 8 metres away from the Echo device or behind a closed door. Whispering does not help — the microphone is very sensitive.
  • Photograph the device. Take a photo of the Echo with a time stamp. Show where it stands and which way it points. This photo is a piece of evidence.
  • Note the recording range. In which rooms can the device record conversations? From where can it pick up sound? Write it down.

Caution: Do not switch the device off and do not unplug it from the power. That could warn the other person and lead to evidence being destroyed.

02

Check the router logs

If you have access to your Wi-Fi router (for example a FritzBox), you can check when the Echo device was active. This is strong evidence.

No router access? That is no problem. You can skip this step and go straight to Step 3. The police can get this data even without your access.

How to find the Echo device in the router:

  • Open your router's interface in the browser (for a FritzBox: fritz.box or 192.168.178.1).
  • In the device list, look for an entry named "Echo" or "Amazon", or an unknown device.
  • Every device has a fixed device number, the MAC address. For Amazon Echo devices it starts with 44:65:0D or 68:37:E9. With this you can identify the device for sure.

Export the logs:

  • Export the connection logs of the Echo device (on a FritzBox: under "System" → "Events").
  • Compare the activity times with times when you were alone and did not use the device on purpose.
  • If the device was active at those times, that is a sign of unauthorised use.

Important: Save all exported files on a USB stick or in a cloud storage that only you can access.

03

Keep a written record

A good record is key for the police and a possible court case. Start an incident diary.

What goes into the diary?

  • The date and time of every suspicious incident.
  • What was talked about? A short summary of the conversation that may have been listened to.
  • How did the person know? Did your flatmate show knowledge that they could only have from listening in?
  • Witnesses: Was anyone else there who can confirm it? Write down their name and contact details.

Tip: It is best to write the diary on paper or in an app on your phone that only you can access. Do not use a shared device or a shared cloud storage.

04

Take legal steps

Once you have saved evidence, you can report it to the police. This is the most important step to stop the listening in.

Before you report:

  • Save all evidence in a safe place: photos, router logs, incident diary.
  • Make a copy of all documents and store it separately (for example a USB stick and a cloud storage).

Report to the police:

  • Go to your nearest police station or use the Online-Wache of your federal state.
  • State the crime as: § 201 StGB ↗ — violation of the confidentiality of the spoken word.
  • Bring all evidence with you (photos, logs, diary).

Why is the report so important? Only the police can legally require Amazon to hand over stored activity data and voice recordings. You yourself can only access the data of your own Amazon account, not the account of the other person.

Also recommended:

  • Get a lawyer for IT law or criminal law. Many offer a free first consultation.
  • Send a DSGVO data request to Amazon: you can ask Amazon for a copy of all data linked to your own account.

Background: how does the spying work?

Amazon Echo devices have a feature called "Drop In". With it, an authorised person can connect to the device at any time and listen in — without a clear signal sounding on the device itself.

In shared flats or in relationships, this feature can be misused to listen in on conversations. Under German law this is a crime.
