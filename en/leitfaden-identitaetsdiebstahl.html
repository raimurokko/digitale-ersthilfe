Identity theft — someone is acting in my name. What to do?

Last reviewed: 12 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: Someone is using your data — ordering things or signing contracts in your name, or setting up a fake profile with your name and your photos. In 4 steps: find out the extent and save evidence, report fake profiles, fight off false claims, report it. You are not to blame.

First aid:

  • Do not pay a claim you do not recognise — not too quickly and not just „to have some peace“.
  • Save evidence (screenshots, letters) before anything gets deleted.
  • Check whether your data comes from a data leak and change affected passwords.
01

Find out the extent and save evidence

  • Save evidence: Screenshots of fake profiles (with the profile address and date), photos of letters, reminders and invoices.
  • Check your creditworthiness: Get a free credit report (a copy of your data under Art. 15 DSGVO ↗) from SCHUFA and other credit agencies (companies that rate whether you can pay) and check the entries.
  • Data leak check: On HPI Identity Leak Checker ↗ or Have I Been Pwned ↗, see which of your data is in circulation.
02

Report fake profiles and have them deleted

  • Report to the network: Report the fake profile (usually via „Report profile“ → „is pretending to be someone“ / identity theft) and demand deletion. The claim follows from your right of personality and name and your right to your own image.
  • Secure your own accounts: Set profiles to private, check passwords and privacy.
  • Warn your contacts: This way friends and family know that messages from the fake profile are not from you.
  • Remove from search: Through the Google form ↗ you can have results with your contact details removed.
03

Fight off false claims

  • Object in writing: Reject unjustified claims and debt collection letters with the sample letter from the Verbraucherzentrale (consumer advice centre) ↗. Do not pay for anything you did not order yourself.
  • Court order for payment? If a court order for payment (Mahnbescheid) arrives, object in time (the deadline is on the order).
  • SCHUFA: Report the identity misuse to SCHUFA and have false entries deleted.
  • Inform the providers: Tell the affected shops and services that the order is not from you.
04

Report it and stay safe

  • Report: to the police or online via the Online-Wache (online police station) ↗. Bring your saved evidence — the report also helps with deletions and fighting off claims.
  • Possible offences: depending on the case § 269 StGB ↗ (forgery of data of evidential value), § 263 StGB ↗ (fraud), for photos § 22/§ 33 KUG ↗ (right to your own image). The police decide how to classify it.
  • Secure your accounts: Change passwords, set up two-factor sign-in, repeat the data leak check.

Frequently asked questions

Do I have to pay a claim I do not recognise?

No, not without checking. Object to unjustified claims in writing — the Verbraucherzentrale offers a free sample letter for this. Only pay for what you can prove you ordered yourself.

A fake profile uses my name and my photos — what can I do?

Report the profile to the network and demand deletion. Save screenshots first and warn your contacts. Photos of you without your consent break your right to your own image (§ 22 KUG ↗).

What about my SCHUFA record?

Get a free credit report (a copy of your data under Art. 15 DSGVO ↗) from SCHUFA and other credit agencies and check the entries. Report the identity misuse to SCHUFA and have false entries deleted.

Where did the offender get my data?

Often from data leaks. Check your email address with the HPI Identity Leak Checker or Have I Been Pwned and change affected passwords.

Is it worth reporting if the person is unknown?

Yes. The report is important as proof — for deletions at platforms and credit agencies and to fight off unjustified claims.
