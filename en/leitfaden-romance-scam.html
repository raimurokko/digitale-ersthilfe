Romance scam — fraud with fake love. What to do?

Last reviewed: 13 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: A person from the internet fakes strong feelings and then asks for money — for emergencies, or a meeting that never happens. This is called a romance or love scam. In 4 steps: stop contact and payments at once, save evidence, tell your bank and report it to the police, protect yourself and get help. It is not your fault.

First aid:

  • Do not send any more money — no matter what emergency is described.
  • Break off contact and stop taking calls or messages.
  • Do not feel ashamed. These are professional scammers — reporting it helps.
01

Stop contact and payments at once

  • No more money: Do not pay anything more — not even "one last time" to get back money you already paid.
  • End contact: Stop replying to messages, calls or new profiles from the same person.
  • Do not let yourself be pressured: Guilt, threats and dramatic "emergencies" are part of the scam.
02

Save evidence

  • Screenshots of the profile and chat history, plus emails and photos you received.
  • Write down names, account and payment details that you sent money to.
  • Save payment receipts (transfers, gift card codes, crypto transactions).
03

Tell your bank and report it to the police

  • Call your bank at once: For a fresh transfer, a recall in the first few hours may still work. If you shared card or account details, block them via 116 116.
  • Report it to the police: at a police station or online via the online police station (Online-Wache) ↗, with the evidence you saved.
  • Possible offence: § 263 StGB ↗ (fraud). The police decide how to classify it.
04

Protect yourself and get help

  • Report the fake profile: Report the profile to the platform (dating site, social network) so it does not hurt others.
  • Beware of "money-back" offers: Anyone who promises to get your lost money back is often the second scam.
  • Talking helps: Losing money and trust is hard. TelefonSeelsorge is there around the clock, free and anonymous (0800 111 0 111).

Frequently asked questions

How do I spot a romance scam?

Typical signs are: fast declarations of love, the person supposedly lives abroad, there is never a real meeting or video call, and then come requests for money for made-up emergencies (illness, customs, travel costs). Often secrecy and payment by bank transfer, gift cards or cryptocurrency are demanded.

Will I get my money back?

Bank transfers usually cannot be reversed — still try at once through your bank (a recall in the first few hours). Payments by gift card or cryptocurrency are normally lost. Reporting it to the police is still important.

Someone offers to get my money back — is that trustworthy?

Usually not. Such „money-back“ offers are often a second scam (recovery scam). Do not respond and do not pay anything.

I feel ashamed — should I still report it?

Yes. It is not your fault — these are professional scammers. Reporting it helps to expose the scam and protect others.

Who helps me if this is hard on me emotionally?

Talking helps. TelefonSeelsorge is available around the clock, free and anonymous (0800 111 0 111). Losing money and trust is a serious burden — get support.
