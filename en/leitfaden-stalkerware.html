Is my phone being watched in secret? Do you suspect stalkerware — what to do?

Last reviewed: 12 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: Stalkerware is a hidden spy app. It can secretly send your location, messages, photos and calls to another person — often a current or former partner. Important first: do not delete anything right away. Removing it can warn the person watching you and make things more dangerous. Get advice first and make a safety plan.

Content note

Content note: If this phone may be watched, look for help from another, safe device. In immediate danger: police 110.

First aid:

  • Warning: If you remove the spy app, change passwords or block the person, the person watching you may notice and become more violent. Act carefully and with help.
  • In immediate danger: emergency call 110. Trust your feeling.
  • Do not talk about your suspicion or your plans on this phone. Use a safe, different device.
  • Get advice before you change anything on the phone (contacts below).
01

Get advice first and save evidence — do not delete anything yet

Talk to a support service first (see „Do you need help?“). Together you make a safety plan: in what order do you act so the person watching you is not alerted?

Save possible evidence. It is best to photograph the screen with a second device, instead of saving screenshots on the suspicious phone. The police can secure evidence properly.

02

Check for signs — calmly and without changing anything

Possible signs (not sure proof, but a hint):

  • The battery drains fast, the phone is often warm.
  • Data use is unusually high.
  • There are apps you do not know.
  • Your current or former partner knows things they cannot know.

The strongest sign is often not technical: someone knows too much about your daily life.

03

Check the device — Android and iPhone separately

Android

  • Play Protect: open the Play Store → your profile picture → „Play Protect“ → scan the device.
  • Device admin apps: Settings → „Security“ → „Device admin apps“. Unknown entries are a warning sign. Spy apps often disguise themselves as an „Accessibility“ service.
  • Go through the app list and permissions (location, microphone, messages).

iPhone (iOS)

  • Safety Check: Settings → „Privacy & Security“ → „Safety Check“ (iOS 16 and later).
  • Configuration profiles / device management: Settings → „General“ → „VPN & Device Management“. Check for unknown profiles.
  • Check location and family sharing in „Find My“; iCloud: is it signed in on an unknown device?

Think about hidden trackers (for example AirTag): phones often warn you automatically; for Android there is the „Tracker Detect“ app. Check your bag, car and jacket.

04

Remove safely — only after the safety plan

  • Change passwords and two-factor sign-in — from a clean, different device. First email, cloud, online banking, social networks; also check recovery details.
  • Safest method: a new phone — almost as good is a reset to factory settings.
  • Be careful with backups: do not restore data from an old backup without checking it — otherwise the app may come back with it.
  • An antivirus app can show stalkerware, but does not always find everything.

Common questions

Why should I not just delete the app right away?

Because the person watching you can notice that the app is gone. That can make them angry and the situation more dangerous. Get advice first and make a plan.

Is it a crime to watch someone in secret like this?

Yes, spying on someone in secret without consent is generally a crime in Germany (for example, §§ 202a, 201a and 238 StGB). The exact classification should be checked by a lawyer.

How can I tell for sure that it is stalkerware?

Telling for sure is hard for non-experts. Battery, heat or unknown apps are only hints. The clearest sign is often that the other person knows too much about you. When in doubt, the police can help secure evidence.

Can stalkerware also be on an iPhone?

Yes. Watch especially for unknown configuration profiles, unfamiliar location and family sharing, and whether your iCloud account is signed in on another device. The „Safety Check“ helps.

What about GPS trackers like AirTags?

Small trackers in a bag or car can also reveal your location. Your phone often warns you automatically about unknown trackers. For Android there is the „Tracker Detect“ app.

I am afraid my suspicion is true. Is it my fault?

No. No one has the right to watch you in secret. It is not your fault, and you are allowed to get help.

Do you need help?

  • Police emergency: 110 — in immediate danger
  • Hilfetelefon — Violence Against Women ↗: 116 016 — free, around the clock, anonymous
  • Weisser Ring ↗ — victim support line: 116 006 — free, every day 7 am–10 pm
  • TelefonSeelsorge: 0800 111 0 111 — free, around the clock
  • Coalition Against Stalkerware: stopstalkerware.org ↗ · local advice via bff (frauen-gegen-gewalt.de) ↗
