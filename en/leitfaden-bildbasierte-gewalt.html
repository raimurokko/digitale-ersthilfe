Someone shared intimate pictures of me, or is threatening to — what can I do?

Last reviewed: 12 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: If someone shares intimate photos or videos of you without your consent, threatens to do so, or makes fake nude pictures (deepfakes), that is violence — and in Germany it is often a crime. You can get the pictures blocked, save evidence and get free advice. It is not your fault.

Content note

Content note: This text talks about sexual violence and blackmail. Read it at your own pace. In immediate danger: police 110. If you share this device with the person who is harming you, use the “Quick exit” button and a device no one can watch.

First aid:

  • It is not your fault. Only the person who shares the pictures or threatens to do so is to blame.
  • If someone blackmails you: do not pay and do not reply. Do not delete anything.
  • Save evidence before you change anything (screenshots with the address/URL, profile name and date — see step 1).
  • In immediate danger: police 110. For a calm talk: HateAid or the Hilfetelefon (numbers below).
01

Save evidence — without forwarding the pictures

  • Take screenshots that show the web address (URL), the profile name, and the date and time.
  • Write down the links to the pages or posts.
  • Do not forward the pictures or videos to anyone — not even to “save evidence”. Forwarding them can itself be a crime.
  • Store everything in a safe place that only you can access.
02

Get pictures blocked and taken down

  • For adults: StopNCII.org. The tool makes a digital “fingerprint” (hash) from your picture right on your device. Only this code is shared, not the picture itself. Participating platforms detect and block matching uploads — also as prevention. Free, from age 18.
  • Were you under 18 when the picture was taken? Then use “Take It Down” (NCMEC). The same hash method, made for pictures of minors. Free.
  • Report directly to the platform (Instagram, TikTok, X, Reddit …) through the report option for “intimate images without consent”.
  • Remove from Google Search: Google has a request form to take intimate personal pictures out of the search results.
03

Think about reporting to the police

  • You can file a report — in person or online through your state’s Online-Wache.
  • Bring the evidence you saved.
  • If someone blackmails you: do not pay, cut off contact, save evidence, report it.

Possible legal grounds include § 201a StGB, § 33 KUG and, for threats, § 253 StGB. Whether they apply in your case needs legal review.

04

Get support and protect your accounts

  • Talk to a support service (numbers below) — free and confidential. The shame is not yours to carry.
  • Protect your accounts: new, strong passwords and two-factor protection. Careful: if someone close to you has access to your devices or accounts, blocking or changing things can alert them — first make a safety plan with a support service.
  • Deepfakes: You can also tackle fake pictures through StopNCII/Take It Down, the platform report and the Google request. In your evidence, note that it is a fake.

Common questions

Is it my own fault because I shared the picture once?

No. Sending a picture willingly is not permission to spread it. The person who passes it on without your consent is responsible.

How does StopNCII help if I do not want to upload the picture?

With StopNCII your picture stays on your device. Only a number code (hash) is made and shared. Participating platforms use it to detect matching uploads and block them.

What about fake nude pictures (deepfakes)?

Fake pictures are an attack on you too. You can have them removed through StopNCII/Take It Down, the platform report and the Google request. In Germany the legal situation is changing right now — get advice.

Is sharing intimate pictures a crime?

Often yes. Possible grounds include § 201a StGB and § 33 KUG; for threats § 253 StGB. Whether a rule applies depends on your case — this is not legal advice.

Do I have to go to the police?

A report is your decision. Either way, you can always get free, confidential advice, for example from HateAid or the Hilfetelefon.

Do you need help?

  • Police emergency: 110 — in immediate danger, around the clock
  • HateAid — advice on digital violence: 030 252 088 38 (opening hours on the website), hateaid.org ↗
  • Hilfetelefon “Violence Against Women” ↗: 116 016 — free, around the clock, in many languages
  • Weisser Ring ↗ — victim support line: 116 006 — free, every day 7 am–10 pm
  • StopNCII (adults): stopncii.org ↗ · Take It Down (minors): takeitdown.ncmec.org ↗
