Shock call with an AI voice (grandparent scam 2.0) — what to do?

Last reviewed: 13 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: A phone call creates panic and demands money fast — supposedly from a relative in trouble or from the "police"/"public prosecutor". The voice can be cloned very convincingly with AI. In 4 steps: stay calm, hang up and call back, check the identity, hand over no money and report it.

First aid:

  • Hang up if someone on the phone creates panic and wants money fast.
  • Call the person back on the number you already know.
  • Never hand over cash or valuables to strangers — not even to "collectors".
01

Stay calm, do not let them pressure you

  • Time pressure is the trick: A serious accident, an arrest or an urgent "bail" is meant to push you into acting fast.
  • Make no decision on the phone and give out no personal data.
  • Do not let them push you to stay on the line or to speak to no one.
02

Hang up and call back

  • Hang up and call the person back on their known, real number (not on a number given to you during the call).
  • A supposed authority? Call the office back on its official number, or dial 110 yourself.
  • If you can, use a different phone to call back — that way the line is sure to be free.
03

Check the identity

  • Ask a question that only the real person can answer. A voice can be cloned — shared knowledge cannot.
  • Family code word: Agree on a word that only you and your relatives know.
  • Warning signs of an AI voice: unnatural pauses, odd stress on words, no background noise.
04

Hand over no money and report it

  • Hand over nothing, transfer nothing: no cash or valuables to couriers, and no payments to unknown accounts.
  • Report it: in an acute scam attempt call 110, otherwise use the Online-Wache (online police station) ↗. Possible offence: § 263 StGB ↗ (fraud) — the police decide how to classify it.
  • Already paid? Call your bank at once (for a recent transfer, try to recall it) and save evidence (time of the call, number).

Frequently asked questions

How do I recognise a shock call?

Typical signs are a dramatic story (a serious accident, an arrest, bail), strong time pressure, a request for money or valuables and being told to speak to no one about it. Often you are pushed not to hang up.

The voice sounded exactly like my child or grandchild — is that possible?

Yes. With artificial intelligence a voice can be cloned very convincingly from just a few seconds of audio (for example from social media videos). Do not rely on the voice alone.

How can I protect myself?

Agree on a code word within your family. On the phone, ask a question that only the real person can answer. If in doubt, hang up and call back on the number you know.

The "police" called and wanted to keep my valuables safe — is that real?

No. The police, the public prosecutor and authorities never ask for cash or valuables on the phone and never send couriers to collect them. Hang up and, if in doubt, dial 110 yourself.

I have already paid or handed something over — what should I do?

Report it at once (if in doubt via 110) and tell your bank — a recent transfer can sometimes be recalled. Save all evidence (time of the call, number, messages).
