Doxing — my private data was published. What can I do?

Last reviewed: 12 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: Someone put your private data (address, phone number, employer, photos) online without your permission — this is called doxing. First things first: save the evidence (screenshots with link and date), get the data deleted and report it to the police. This is not your fault.

Content note

Content note: In immediate danger — for example if someone threatens you or is at your door — call the police 110 right away.

First aid:

  • Save evidence before you report anything — screenshots with URL, date, time.
  • Do not share it further yourself and do not argue with the offenders, do not answer threats.
  • If there are threats to life or limb: police 110.
  • You are not alone. Free advice from HateAid; if you feel very afraid, the TelefonSeelsorge (0800 111 0 111).
01

Save evidence

  • Take a screenshot of every place you find your data — with the full URL, date and time.
  • Note where the data appears (platform, username).
  • Store the screenshots safely (for example a USB stick or a protected cloud folder).
  • Calmly look for more places (for example type your own name into a search engine).
02

Get it deleted

  • Report it to the platform (almost every one has a "Report" button).
  • Ask the website owner to delete it (DSGVO): You have a right to erasure under Art. 17 DSGVO ↗. The Verbraucherzentrale (consumer advice centre) offers a free sample letter.
  • Remove it from Google Search: Google has a "Results about you" form for contact details. This makes the entry harder to find in Search; Google does not delete the original website itself.
03

Improve your own safety

  • Secure your accounts: change passwords, turn on two-factor sign-in (2FA).
  • Check your privacy: set profiles to private, remove public details (address, phone, employer).
  • In serious danger: apply for an information block in the population register (§ 51 BMG ↗) at the residents' registration office (clarify this with the authority or an advice centre).

Be careful with shared devices/accounts: If the offender has access, changing passwords may be noticed — get advice first and make a safety plan.

04

Report it to the police

  • Report it to the police or through the Online-Wache (online police station) of your federal state (upload screenshots).
  • Bring your saved evidence.

Possible offences: § 126a StGB (dangerous distribution of personal data, since 2021), § 185 and following (insult and more), § 238 StGB (stalking). Which offence applies is decided by the police. Free advice: HateAid.

Common questions

What is doxing anyway?

Doxing means: someone collects private data about you and publishes it without permission — address, phone number, employer, photos. Often the goal is to call on others to harass you.

Why save evidence first and not report it right away?

If a post is deleted, it is often no longer findable as evidence. With screenshots (URL and date) you secure the proof for a later report. Save first, then report.

Can I ask for my data to be deleted?

Yes. Under Art. 17 DSGVO ↗ you have a right to erasure. You can write to the website owner and also have entries removed from Search with the Google form.

Is doxing a crime?

Yes, doxing can be a crime. Since 2021 there is § 126a StGB ↗ ("dangerous distribution of personal data"). Depending on the case, other offences may apply too. The police decide how it is classified.

What if I am being harassed or threatened right now?

In immediate danger, call 110 at once. Do not answer threats. Block and report the accounts (screenshots first). Talking helps: HateAid or the TelefonSeelsorge (0800 111 0 111).

Do I have to keep my address secret forever now?

Not necessarily. In serious danger you can apply for an information block in the population register (§ 51 BMG ↗) — time-limited at first, renewable. Whether it fits your case, clarify with your registration office or an advice centre.

Do you need help?

  • Police emergency: 110 — in immediate danger
  • HateAid — digital violence: 030 252 088 38 (phone times Mon, Tue, Thu; exact times and appointments on hateaid.org), hateaid.org ↗
  • TelefonSeelsorge: 0800 111 0 111 — free, anonymous, around the clock
  • Hilfetelefon "Violence Against Women" ↗: 116 016 — free, around the clock
  • Online-Wache (online police station): onlinewache.polizei.de ↗
  • Verbraucherzentrale: sample letter for deletion under Art. 17 DSGVO — verbraucherzentrale.de ↗
