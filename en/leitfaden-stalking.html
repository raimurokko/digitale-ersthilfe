Stalking — what to do?

Last reviewed: 13 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: Stalking is repeated following, lying in wait, unwanted contact or surveillance. It is a crime under § 238 StGB. Act in 4 steps: keep a diary, apply for a protection order, stop digital surveillance, report it to the police.

First aid:

  • Make it clear, one time and plainly, that you want no contact — then do not reply again.
  • Do not block the person right away everywhere — you need the messages as evidence. Use "mute" or a separate folder.
  • If you are in immediate danger: police 110. For advice around the clock: Hilfetelefon 08000 116 016.
01

Keep a stalking diary

A complete diary is the most important basis for a protection order and a police report. Write down every incident — even ones that seem harmless.

Note for each entry:

  • Date and time (exact)
  • Place of the incident
  • What happened? — Plainly, in your own words
  • Who was involved? — Witnesses with name and contact, if possible
  • How did you feel? — Fear, sleep problems, physical reactions
  • Effects on daily life — changed your way to work, cancelled appointments

Also secure evidence:

  • Screenshots of messages (SMS, messenger, social media) with a visible date and sender.
  • Export or photograph your call logs.
  • Keep letters, cards and gifts — do not throw them away.
  • If there is property damage or lying in wait: take photos right away.

Tip: Weisser Ring offers a free stalking diary as a PDF download ↗. The police also explain how to document incidents ↗.

02

Apply for a protection order

The Violence Protection Act (Gewaltschutzgesetz, GewSchG) allows fast civil protection orders — no matter whether you report the crime.

Possible orders:

  • Contact ban: no calls, messages, letters or contact through others.
  • Stay-away order: keep distance from your home, workplace, school (for example 100 m).
  • No-go order: certain places may no longer be visited.
  • Assignment of the home: if you share a home.

How to proceed:

  • Apply to the family court where you live — this works even without a lawyer.
  • Support services (for example Weisser Ring, women's counselling centres) help you fill it in.
  • In urgent cases there is an emergency order within a few days.
  • If you have a low income: apply for court cost aid (Verfahrenskostenhilfe).
  • Breaches are a crime under § 4 GewSchG — report every breach to the police right away.
03

Stop digital surveillance

Stalkers often use digital tools today. Check step by step whether devices, accounts or objects have been changed or taken over in secret.

Check your smartphone:

  • Stalkerware (secret spy apps): is the battery drain, unknown apps or high data use unusual? Android: Settings → Apps → show all. iOS: Settings → General → VPN & Device Management.
  • Check your location sharing (Google Maps, Apple "Find My", Find My Device, Family Sharing).
  • When in doubt: reset to factory settings, and restore only selected data from the cloud.

Tracking devices in the car and your bags:

  • Your phone can detect unknown AirTags/Samsung tags/Tile: iPhone does it automatically, Android through the "Tracker detection" app (Google) or "AirGuard".
  • Car: check under the vehicle, the wheel wells, the boot and the diagnostic port (OBD connector) — when in doubt, go to a garage.

Accounts and smart home:

  • Change all passwords, turn on 2FA — see the guide My account was hacked.
  • If you suspect listening through Amazon Echo / Google Nest / smart home: guide Spotting surveillance through Amazon Echo.
  • Set social media profiles to private, turn off location tags in photos.
  • Disconnect shared cloud storage, iCloud Family Sharing, Spotify Family and so on.

Important: Remove stalkerware only after you have secured evidence (a screenshot of the app entry, the time, the data use). Otherwise the surveillance is hard to prove later.

04

Report it to the police

Stalking (Nachstellung) is a crime under § 238 StGB ↗ — punishable by up to 3 years in prison, and up to 5 years in serious cases.

  • Report it to the police in person or online through the Online-Wache ↗.
  • Bring with you: stalking diary ↗, screenshots, a storage device with evidence, contacts of witnesses.
  • Other crimes that may apply: § 185 StGB (insult), § 201a StGB (unlawful photos), § 202a StGB (data spying), § 223 StGB (bodily harm), § 241 StGB (threats).
  • Weisser Ring ↗ (116 006) will go with you to the police and the court if you wish, and helps with counselling vouchers.
  • Victim compensation under SGB XIV ↗ (since 2024) is possible if you have health consequences.

Frequently asked questions

When is stalking a crime?

Stalking — repeated following, lying in wait, unwanted contact or surveillance — is a crime under § 238 StGB ↗. A record of the incidents that is as complete as possible is the basis for a protection order and a police report.

Should I block the stalker right away?

Make it clear one time that you want no contact, and do not respond after that. But do not block them everywhere right away — you need the messages as evidence. Use "mute" or a separate folder instead.

How do I keep a stalking diary?

For each incident, write down the date, time, place, what happened, any witnesses, your feelings and the effects on your daily life. Also save screenshots with date and sender, call logs, and any letters and gifts you keep. Free help: Weisser Ring offers a free stalking diary as a PDF ↗; the police also explain how to keep records ↗.

How can I get protection fast, even without a police report?

Under the Violence Protection Act ↗ you can ask the family court responsible for you ↗ for a civil protection order — for example a contact ban, a stay-away order or a no-go order — even without a lawyer and no matter whether you report the crime. In urgent cases there is an emergency order within a few days; if you have a low income, court cost aid can help.

How do I spot digital surveillance like stalkerware or tracking?

Watch for unusual battery drain, unknown apps and high data use, check your location sharing, and look for unknown trackers like AirTags (iPhone finds them automatically; on Android use "Tracker detection" or the free app "AirGuard" from TU Darmstadt, also for Android ↗). Change all your passwords and turn on 2FA; when in doubt, reset the device to factory settings — be careful, this can alert the person watching you (see the Stalkerware guide). See also the guides My account was hacked and Spotting surveillance through Amazon Echo.
