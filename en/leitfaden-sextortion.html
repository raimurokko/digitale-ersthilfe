Someone threatens to publish my intimate images and demands money — what to do?

Last reviewed: 13 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: This is called sextortion (sexual blackmail): A person threatens to publish intimate photos, videos or webcam recordings if you do not send money or more images. Do not pay, do not delete anything, save evidence and report it to the police. It is not your fault — the fault lies with the person who is blackmailing you.

Content note

Content note: This text talks about sexual blackmail and intimate recordings. You do not have to read everything at once. In immediate danger: police 110. If you share this device with the person who is harming you, use the “Quick exit” button and an unwatched device.

First aid:

  • Do not pay any money. A payment usually does not stop the blackmail — often new demands follow.
  • Do not delete anything. Chats, messages and the person's profile are important evidence.
  • Do not reply anymore. Break off contact. Do not negotiate.
  • In immediate danger: police 110.
01

Stay calm and break off contact

Take a short breath. You do not have to react quickly now — the opposite is true. Do not reply to the person anymore. Do not give in to demands and send no money and no more images. Experience shows that payments do not bring the blackmail to an end.

02

Save evidence (before you report or block)

First save evidence before you report or block the profile. Take screenshots of:

  • the person's profile (name, profile picture, link/address of the profile),
  • the whole chat history and the threats,
  • the payment demand (for example bank account, crypto address, PayPal, amount),
  • the username and — if visible — the date and time.

Keep the images safe. But do not share the material any further.

03

Report the profile, block it and secure your own accounts

Only after you have saved evidence: Report the profile to the platform operator and block the contact. Ask the operator to delete any published content. After that, set your own accounts to be safer (friend/follower lists and personal details on private), so the person cannot reach the people around you.

04

Report it to the police

File a report — at any police station or online through the Onlinewache (online police station) of your federal state. Bring your saved evidence with you. Sextortion can meet the criminal offence of blackmail (§ 253 StGB ↗) — the exact legal assessment is made by the police or the public prosecutor.

Special case: blackmail email “I filmed you through your webcam”

Most of the time this is pure bluff. Such mass emails claim that someone filmed you through your webcam while you were watching porn, and they demand money (often in Bitcoin). As a rule there is no video, your device was not hacked and your account was not taken over.

This is what you should do:

  • Do not pay — not even in cryptocurrency.
  • Do not reply and do not open any links or attachments. Mark it as spam.
  • Is a real (old) password of yours in the email? Then it usually comes from a data leak. Change this password everywhere you still use it, and turn on two-factor login. Check at HPI Identity Leak Checker ↗ or Have I Been Pwned ↗ which data is affected.
  • You can report the incident — to the police or the online police station, or to the Verbraucherzentrale (consumer advice centre) ↗.

Important: This special case is about threat emails without real material. But if real intimate recordings of you were actually used for the blackmail, follow the steps above in this guide.

Common questions

Should I pay so it stops?

No. A payment usually does not end the blackmail — often more demands follow. Do not pay.

I sent the images myself — is it now my fault?

No. Trusting someone is not forbidden. The person who blackmails you with them is committing the crime — not you.

Should I block the profile right away?

Only block after you have saved evidence. Then: block and report.

They say my camera was hacked and I was filmed — is that true?

Often this is an empty threat sent by mass email, with no real material — especially when only text comes and an old password is named. Still, do not pay. Change affected passwords and report the email.

What if a minor is affected?

This is especially serious. Get help from a support service (for example Nummer gegen Kummer) and file a report. Through the service “Take It Down”, intimate recordings of minors can be blocked worldwide (the images are not uploaded).

What if the images have already been published?

Save that as evidence too, report it to the platform to have it removed, and get advice (for example HateAid). Publications can be reported and removed.

Do you need help?

  • Police emergency: 110 — in immediate danger, around the clock
  • HateAid — advice on digital violence: 030 252 088 38 (see website for hours), hateaid.org ↗
  • Weisser Ring ↗ — victim helpline: 116 006 — free, every day 7 am–10 pm
  • Nummer gegen Kummer ↗ (children/young people): 116 111 — free, anonymous
  • Hilfetelefon Sexueller Missbrauch (help line on sexual abuse): 0800 22 55 530 — free, anonymous
  • StopNCII (adults) — have images blocked in advance without uploading them: stopncii.org ↗
  • Take It Down (for minors): takeitdown.ncmec.org ↗
