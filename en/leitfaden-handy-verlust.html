Phone lost or stolen — what now?

Last reviewed: 12 July 2026 · updated: 13 July 2026

In short: Is your smartphone gone? Act fast — and never go after a thief yourself. In 4 steps: find and lock it from far away, block the SIM card, secure accounts and payment services, report it.

First aid:

  • Never go after a thief yourself. If it was stolen with violence or a threat, call the police 110 right away.
  • Lock it from far away first, before you block the SIM — otherwise the lock or erase command may no longer reach the device.
  • Change your email account password first — many other accounts depend on it.
01

Find and lock it from far away

Sign in to your account from another device (a computer, tablet or the phone of someone you trust). This lets you find your device, lock it and show a message with a way to contact you.

  • iPhone (Apple): At iCloud.com/find ↗ choose the device and „Mark As Lost“ (Lost Mode). This locks the iPhone, shows a message and pauses Apple Pay. (Apple Help ↗)
  • Android (Google): At android.com/find ↗ („Find My Device“ / Find Hub) find the device, make it ring, lock it and show a message.
  • If it was stolen: Note the location, but leave finding it to the police.

Tip: These features must be turned on beforehand — „Find My“ on Apple, „Find My Device“ on Android. For the future: check once that they are switched on.

02

Get the SIM card blocked

Block the SIM card so no one can make calls at your cost and no SMS codes (two-factor login) can be intercepted.

  • Through your mobile provider: Call the customer hotline (the number is in your contract or on the provider's website) — this works for prepaid cards too.
  • Or through the blocking hotline 116 116: it blocks SIM cards of participating providers, around the clock, free from German landlines.

Mind the order: If you want to erase sensitive data from far away (step 4), start that while the device can still be reached online — and only block the SIM afterwards.

03

Secure accounts, cards and payment services

  • Email first: Change your email account password, then your other important accounts. Sign out „all devices“ everywhere.
  • Payment services: Apple Pay and Google Pay are paused in Lost Mode. Ask your bank to block access to your banking app.
  • Block bank and credit cards: through your bank's emergency number or the blocking hotline 116 116.
  • Two-factor login: If codes or an authenticator app were on the phone, set them up again for your important accounts (use your backup codes).
04

Report it and erase your data

  • Report it: at any police station or online through the Online-Wache der Polizei (police online station) ↗. For the report you need the IMEI (15 digits) and proof of purchase.
  • Find the IMEI: on the box and invoice, in your Google/Apple account — or on the device with the key combination *#06#.
  • Possible offences: § 242 StGB ↗ (theft); with violence or a threat § 249 StGB ↗ (robbery). The police decide how to classify it.
  • Erase your data (last step): If you do not get the device back and sensitive data is on it, erase it from far away (Lost Mode → erase). After that it can no longer be found.
  • For insurance: Keep the case number of the report for your household or phone insurance.

Common questions

Should I go and look for my phone myself?

No, especially not if it was stolen. Leave finding it to the police. If there is violence or a threat, call 110 right away.

Do I need to block the SIM if the phone is locked with a PIN?

Yes. The SIM card can be put into another device — then calls are made at your cost and SMS codes (two-factor login) can be intercepted. Block the SIM with your provider or through 116 116.

What is the IMEI and where do I find it?

The IMEI is the 15-digit serial number of your device. It is on the box and the invoice, in your Google or Apple account, and you can show it on the device with the key combination *#06#. The police need it for the report.

Should I erase the data from far away right now?

First try to find and lock the device — erasing ends the tracking. If you do not get the device back and sensitive data is on it, erase it from far away, ideally while it can still be reached online.

What if my 2FA codes or authenticator app were on the phone?

Secure your important accounts again from another device: change the password, set up new two-factor login and use your backup codes. Start with your email account.
